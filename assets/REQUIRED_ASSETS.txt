EQUITY SPRINT ONLINE — Required Property Photo Assets
======================================================

These images are used as the hero image on marketplace property cards.
Placeholder CSS gradients are used until real photos are sourced.

Once photos are added:
  - Place each file in this /assets/ folder
  - Name the file exactly as listed below
  - The client will auto-detect and replace the gradient placeholder

------------------------------------------------------
SPECIFICATIONS
------------------------------------------------------
  Format:   JPEG or WebP (preferred)
  Size:     400 × 300 px minimum (will be cropped to 16:9 ratio on card)
  Style:    Exterior shot of the property — no people, no text overlays
  Tone:     Realistic Australian residential/commercial photography
            Clean natural lighting. Day shots preferred.
            Should look like a real estate listing photo.

------------------------------------------------------
REQUIRED FILES (one per property type)
------------------------------------------------------

  unit.jpg
    → A modern apartment unit exterior in a mid-density building
    → e.g. 3–5 storey brick or render apartment, street level view
    → Represents: Townsville Unit, Rockhampton Unit, Adelaide Unit,
                  Perth Unit, Brisbane Unit, Cairns Unit

  house.jpg
    → A detached family home with front garden/driveway
    → e.g. Queensland Queenslander or Federation style, wide frontage
    → Represents: Sydney, Brisbane, Perth, Adelaide, Canberra houses
                  and most regional houses (Ballarat, Toowoomba, etc.)

  warehouse.jpg
    → An inner-city converted warehouse or industrial-chic building
    → e.g. exposed brick, large windows, Melbourne laneway aesthetic
    → Represents: Melbourne warehouse

  apartment.jpg
    → A high-rise or mid-rise apartment building, coastal setting
    → e.g. Gold Coast waterfront tower or modern glassy building
    → Represents: Gold Coast apartment

  cottage.jpg
    → A heritage or character cottage — weatherboard or sandstone
    → e.g. Hobart colonial cottage or Launceston heritage home
    → Represents: Hobart cottage, Launceston cottage, Byron Bay Cottage

  duplex.jpg
    → A side-by-side duplex in a leafy suburban street
    → e.g. Sydney North Shore or Brisbane gentrified suburb
    → Represents: Lane Cove Duplex

  terrace.jpg
    → A Victorian or Edwardian terrace row
    → e.g. Brisbane Teneriffe or Sydney inner-west terrace
    → Represents: Teneriffe Terrace

  heritage-house.jpg
    → An art deco or interwar prestige home
    → e.g. Melbourne South Yarra statement home, well-maintained
    → Represents: South Yarra Heritage

  beach-house.jpg
    → A beachfront or ocean-view home
    → e.g. Perth Cottesloe limestone or Hamptons-style beach house
    → Represents: Cottesloe Beach House

  estate.jpg
    → A large rural property with acreage — acreage home or homestead
    → e.g. Noosa hinterland — lush green, rural backdrop
    → Represents: Noosa Hinterland Estate

  penthouse.jpg
    → A premium apartment building exterior from a low angle
       showing rooftop or sky setting, Sydney CBD or Bondi backdrop
    → Represents: Bondi Penthouse

  manor.jpg
    → A grand Melbourne prestige mansion — Toorak style
    → e.g. double-storey period home with manicured gardens, iron gates
    → Represents: Toorak Manor

------------------------------------------------------
OPTIONAL: City-specific overrides
------------------------------------------------------
If you want a unique image for a specific city (e.g. just Sydney),
add a file named after the city slug:
  sydney.jpg, melbourne.jpg, bondi-penthouse.jpg, toorak-manor.jpg, etc.
(spaces replaced with hyphens, all lowercase)
The client checks for a city-specific image before falling back to propType.
